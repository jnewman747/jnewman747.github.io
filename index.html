Wildfires in Alaska

Wildfires are unplanned fire that burn in a natural area and can be caused either anthropogenically or naturally (i.e., lightning). This static heatmap illustrates wildfires in Alaska between the years of 1992 and 2020. [This map was created in QGIS using spatial joins and hexbins]

Natural Landforms in Alaska

Natural arches are natural landforms (i.e., sandstone or limestone) typically associated with inland and coastal cliffs and are vulnerable to erosion. Alaska's Natural Arch can be located within Wrangell-Saint Elias National Park at 3700 feet. [This map was created using GDAL, QGIS, and Mapbox]

Predicted Habitat

Climate change is a threat to many species, particularly amphibians. At Clemson University, we conducted a lab experiment to determine water loss rates of green salamanders. The results informed areas of potential future refugia under different climate scenarios. More information about this map and the associated manuscript can be found here. [This map was created using ArcGIS and MaxEnt]

United States Energy Sources

There are three main categories for sources of electricity: fossil fuels, nuclear, and renewable energy. This interactive map represents three distinct sources of electricity in the United States and provides information about the power plants generating the energy. [This map was created using Leaflet]

About Me

I'm currently a Research Analyst for the University of Kentucky. My research focuses on conservation of threatened and endangered reptiles and amphibians. I earned my B.S. in Biology from Northeastern University (2013) and M.S. in Wildlife and Fisheries Biology from Clemson University (2017). I have worked across many different and exciting ecoregions of the United States including the White Mountains of New Hampshire, Kentucky coal fields, and Alabama coastal plains. My inspiration for mapping stems from my graduate studies and my travels across the United States.

When I'm not playing in the woods, you can find me playing tennis, playing the saxophone, or watching Boston sports.
