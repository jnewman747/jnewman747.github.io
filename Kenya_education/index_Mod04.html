Kenyan Education

Enrollment in Kenyan Primary Schools

In 2013, Uhuru Kenyatta of the National Alliance party was elected President of Kenya. As part of Kenyatta's plan, he pledged to address Kenya's "learning crisis" through the Digital Literacy Program. The Digital Literacy Program brought electronics (i.e., laptops) into the classroom. Kenya's educational system significantly rose in the rankings since its installation.

This interactive map provides the number of both enrolled male and female Kenyan students by county in the year 2014. Primary school in Kenya is considered the first stage of education for a child. Children ages 5-12 participate in primary education.
