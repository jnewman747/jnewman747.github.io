Natural Arch

Jill Newman; University of Kentucky Geography

The Natural Arch is located in southeastern Alaska and is part of the Wrangell-Saint Elias National Park. It is found in Valdez-Cordova Census Area, Alaska and got its name in 1913 by the US Geological Survey. Its elevation is 3711 feet. Arches extracted from the Domestic and Antarctic Names (State and Topical Gazetteer) published by the United States Board on Geographic Namkes. (geonames.usgs.gov) Data accessed August, 2018. Contours were extracted from a USGS NED 1/3 arc-second raster digital elevation model. This data can be accessed at the USGS 3D Elevation Program (usgs.gov)

Tools used in this project were Select by Expression, Extraction, Clip, Raster Calculator, Contour, and Field Calculator

  • PostGIS was used to store/query the national dataset.
  • Data is shown on Mapbox web map with geolocation services enabled. If the map got popular it would require a fee to use.
  • GDAL and QGIS to convert raster elevation units and create contours.
